Africa

Africa is the second largest continent and population in the world. Islam is the most pupularf religion in Africa. Africa has the shortest coastline even though its the second largest continent in the world.
While Arabic is the most spoken language in Africa, there's plenty more languages including Igbo, Yoruba, Amharic, Portuguese, Oromo, Berber, Zulu and Shona.
Africa is where modern humans originated from. The past 200,000 years Humans originated most likely in the area around the Kenyan Rift Valley, where the oldest known stone tools were discovered.

There is only 2 types of camels in the world. There are one humped camels and two humped Bactrian camels. Camels have thick lips which let them eat thorny plants other animals can't eat. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

There were really large camel caravans that travelled the minimum 1000 kilometres to cross the entire Sahara desert.

The Sahara Desert is mostly rock and not sand! The sahara desert is just 30% sand, the remaining 70% being mostly gravel. The rest of the desert comprises sand, seas, salt flats, valleys, mountains, rivers, streams.



The landscape is a combination of nutritious grasslands and sparse woodlands: grassy areas dotted with Acacia trees, the iconic image of the Serengeti. Around 2 million large plant-eating mammals live in the savanna.

African elephants measure 3 meters tall and weigh up to 6 tons. They can live up to 70 years old. There are African elephants and Asian elephants. African elephants have an elongated ear.

The Serengeti Plains are a grass savanna that is very dry but has nutrient-rich volcanic sand. Around 2 million large plant-eating mammals live in the savanna.

Malawi

The region was once part of the Maravi Empire. In colonial times, the territory was ruled by the British, under whose control it was known first as British Central Africa. Malawi a landlocked country in southeastern Africa, has spectacular highlands and extensive lakes, curving strip of land along the African Rift Valley. Lake Malawi, accounts for more than one-fifth of the country’s total area.

Malawi is about 840 km from north to south and is between 10 and 160 km in width. It is bordered by 3 countries and 1 lake, Tanzania, Mozambique, Zambia, and Lake Malawi.

A Malawian dish

Kachumbari, a type of tomato and onion salad, known locally in Malawi as a sumu or shum.

Below is the dish Kachumbari

Ten major ethnic groups are historically associated with modern Malawi. These groups are the Chewa, Nyanja, Lomwe, Yao, Tumbuka, Sena, Tonga, Ngoni, Ngonde, and the Lambya. All the African languages spoken are Bantu languages.
