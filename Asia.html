Asia

Ferdinand Magellan left Spain with five ships and 270 men in 1519, Ferdinand Magellan was the first to reach Asia from the East. (1519).
The Mongol Empire existed during the 13th and 14th centuries and it is recognized as being the largest land empire in history. Led by Genghis Khan and his sons and grandsons, the Mongols briefly ruled most of modern-day Russia, China, Korea, southeast Asia, Persia, India, the Middle East and eastern Europe. They reshaped world geography, culture and history in ways that still resound today.

There are 48 countries in Asia. More than 4.6 billion people live on the continent. Asia is currently the most populated continent.

Insects are eaten as delicacies in some Asian countries.

Asia is the largest continent in the world and home to nearly five billion people and roughly 2,300 languages.

Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge

The world's longest bridge is the Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge in China The bridge, which opened in June 2011, is around 165 kilometers (102.4 miles). China constructed the Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge in just four years, employing 10,000 workers, at a cost of about $8.5 million, according to Encyclopaedia Britannica. It crosses low rice paddies, part of the Yangtze River Delta, with just a few miles of the bridge actually crossing the open water of Yangcheng Lake in Suzhou. The bridge averages about 100 feet (31 meters) off the ground.
