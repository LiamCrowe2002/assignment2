Europe

In Europe, Christianity has always been the dominant religion. Today, however, more and more people on the continent adhere to irreligion (don't have religious beliefs).
The earliest appearance of anatomically modern people in Europe has been dated to 35,000 BC, usually referred to as the Cro-Magnon. The earliest sites in Europe are Italy, Germany, and France.
The Treaty of Rome, or EEC Treaty, brought about the creation of the European Economic Community (EEC). Some 35 years later the European Union was formed. The EEC was designed to create a common market among its members through the elimination of most trade barriers and the establishment of a common external trade policy and the European Union was formed, with the aim of ending the frequent and bloody wars between neighbours. The EU was formed on the November 1, 1993.

One euro is made up of 100 cents. Coins are minted in denominations of 1, 2, 5, 10, 20 and 50 cents, and 1 and 2 euros.

Of the European countries, 27 nations belong to the European Union (EU) which is a political and economic union

There is almost 30 car brands in Europe, 1 Sweden, Germany 6, UK 7, Italy 5, France 4, Sweden 4, Spain 1, Russia 1, and Czech Republic 1.

Europe is the second smallest continent in size but the third largest in population.

The largest country in Europe, seems to be debatable between Ukraine or Russia. However here we make you decide which country you think is the king of Europe.

The highest peak of the Caucasus and the highest point in Europe is Mount Elbrus in southwestern Russia. Formed more than 2.5 million years ago, this extinct volcano has twin cones that extend to elevations of 18,510 feet (5,642 meters) and 5,595 meters (18,356 feet).

Germans famously love cars, and they love to set them free to show what they can do. As a result, there are no speed limits on around 65% of the Autobahn network.

Beer plays a big role in German life, and it has done for generations. In fact, the world's oldest brewery is German. Weihenstephan Abbey has been brewing up its own varieties since 1040. Germans also consume 104 liters of beer every year – ranking them 4th in the world.

Sweet maker Haribo runs a scheme which lets local children exchange acorns they have collected for delicious gummy sweets. The acorns are sent to nature reserves for animals to feast upon.
